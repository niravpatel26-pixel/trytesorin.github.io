FAQs · Questions about TESORIN

Answers to common questions about TESORIN.

This page collects the most frequent questions people ask before joining the early access list — what TESORIN is, how it works, and how it fits into your money life.

Short version: TESORIN is a calm planning app for people taking money seriously for the first time — not a trading screen, not a hype machine.

Getting started

Basics.

What TESORIN is, who it’s for, and how to know if it’s worth your time.

Do I need a lot of money to start using TESORIN?
No. TESORIN is deliberately built for small, realistic starting points. The question is not “how much do you have?” — it’s “what can we build from here?”.
Is TESORIN an investing or trading app?
TESORIN is a planning app, not a trading screen. It focuses on your cash flow, buffers, debt, and long-term wealth path. Any investing happens later, as an extension of a solid plan — and usually through platforms you already use or choose separately.
Which countries does TESORIN support first?
Early guidance is tuned for common scenarios in India and Canada — salaries, taxes, and day-to-day money decisions that people actually face there. As TESORIN grows, the roadmap includes expanding to more regions with equally thoughtful context.
When will I be able to actually use the app?
A first early-access version is being prepared. People on the waitlist will be invited in small waves so the product can be shaped with real feedback before a wider release. If you want to be part of that, join from the homepage.
Money & decisions

What TESORIN actually does.

How TESORIN helps you move from “I should plan my money” to “I have a plan I can stick to”.

Will TESORIN tell me exactly what to invest in?
TESORIN will not shout stock tips at you. Its job is to show how much you can safely put towards investing, what that money is for, and how it fits into your broader life. Specific investment choices are left to you or to separate, regulated platforms you trust.
What will I see when I log in?
A calm snapshot of your money life: income, rough spending, what’s left over, and how that surplus is currently split between safety, debt, and long-term wealth. From there, TESORIN suggests small, realistic changes — not complete life overhauls every month.
Can TESORIN replace a human financial planner?
For many people just getting serious about money, TESORIN can cover the basics a planner would: clear priorities, a sense of “enough”, and concrete monthly amounts. If your situation becomes complex (businesses, multiple properties, tricky tax planning), TESORIN is meant to sit alongside a human advisor, not replace them.
Practicalities

Cost, data, and access.

A few straightforward answers about pricing, data handling, and how TESORIN plans to stay on your side.

How much will TESORIN cost?
Early-access versions are planned to be free while the product is being shaped with real users. Future pricing will be shared clearly and ahead of time, with a goal of keeping the core planning experience accessible and simple to understand.
How does TESORIN make money if it isn’t selling me products?
The long-term plan is a mix of transparent subscription options and carefully chosen partnerships that don’t compromise user trust. The principle is simple: no hidden commissions, no “recommendations” driven by sales targets.
What about my data?
TESORIN is being designed with a “respect first” approach to data. That means collecting only what’s needed for planning, using secure storage, and avoiding selling identifiable data to third parties. As the product gets closer to launch, a clear, readable privacy policy — not a wall of legalese — will spell this out.
