FAQs · Questions about TESORIN

Answers to common questions about TESORIN.

This page collects the most frequent questions people ask before joining the early access list — what TESORIN is, how it works, and how it fits into your money life.

Short version: TESORIN is a calm planning app for people taking money seriously for the first time — not a trading screen, not a hype machine.

Money & decisions

What TESORIN actually does.

How TESORIN helps you move from “I should plan my money” to “I have a plan I can stick to”.

TESORIN doesn’t give stock tips. Its job is to show how much you can safely put towards investing, what that money is for, and how it fits into your broader life. Specific investment choices are left to you or to separate, regulated platforms you trust.
A calm snapshot of your money life: income, rough spending, what’s left over, and how that surplus is currently split between safety, debt, and long-term wealth. From there, TESORIN suggests small, realistic changes — not complete life overhauls every month.
For many people just getting serious about money, TESORIN can cover the basics a planner would: clear priorities, a sense of “enough”, and concrete monthly amounts. If your situation becomes complex (businesses, multiple properties, tricky tax planning), TESORIN is meant to sit alongside a human advisor, not replace them.
Practicalities

Cost, data, and access.

Straightforward answers about pricing, data handling, and how TESORIN plans to stay on your side.

Early-access versions are planned to be free while the product is being shaped with real users. Future pricing will be shared clearly and ahead of time, with a goal of keeping the core planning experience accessible and simple to understand.
The long-term plan is a mix of transparent subscription options and carefully chosen partnerships that don’t compromise user trust. The principle is simple: no hidden commissions, no “recommendations” driven by sales targets.
TESORIN is being designed with a “respect first” approach to data. That means collecting only what’s needed for planning, using secure storage, and avoiding selling identifiable data to third parties. As the product gets closer to launch, a clear, readable privacy policy will spell this out.
