The flow

Five steps, repeated. No rebuilds.

The goal isn’t precision. It’s a plan that stays stable under real life.

Start with what’s most stable:
  • Income and fixed commitments.
  • Baseline spending and bills.
  • Balances: savings, debt, and any investments you already have.
Add detail only when it improves decisions.

TESORIN gives you an anchor number: what’s available after essentials, without weakening your baseline. It’s designed to be actionable, not theoretical.

Instead of juggling everything at once, you pick the most useful move for your situation — the one that reduces fragility or increases momentum.

Actions are intentionally small:
  • A single transfer, payoff, or target you can keep.
  • A short “why” so it’s clear what this improves.
  • A simple check-in, not daily tracking.

Rent change, travel, family support, a raise — you update the snapshot and the plan recalculates. The loop stays the same; the numbers adapt.
