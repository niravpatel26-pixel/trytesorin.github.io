Trust comes from boundaries.

TESORIN is designed to stay in a conservative lane: planning, clarity, and control — without moving your money or steering you toward products.

Important: TESORIN provides planning support and education. It isn’t personal financial, legal, or tax advice.

Safety

What “safe by design” means here.

Open the sections you care about: boundaries, money handling, data, limitations, and simple anti-scam checks.

TESORIN is a planning layer that helps you map priorities over time. It’s designed to reduce pressure, not increase it.

TESORIN is:
  • A clear view of cash flow, buffers, debt direction, and goals.
  • A sequence that helps you choose the next action — month by month.
  • Built for people starting to take planning seriously, without spreadsheet maintenance.
TESORIN is not:
  • A bank, brokerage, or wallet that holds funds.
  • A system that transfers money or places trades in the background.
  • A sales channel designed around commissions.
  • Personal advice tailored to your full legal/tax situation.
Planning lane No custody No product pressure

TESORIN starts with a conservative assumption: your money stays with the providers you already use.
  • No custody: TESORIN doesn’t hold your funds.
  • No hidden movement: it doesn’t transfer money or place trades for you.
  • Explicit actions: when you act, it’s a step you take through your bank or broker.
  • Cautious integrations: if account connections are added later, you’ll see what’s shared and what it enables.
Your plan can be useful without your money leaving regulated rails.

Trust requires restraint. TESORIN is designed to avoid collecting data “just in case.”
  • Minimum necessary: enough to plan, not to profile.
  • Plain-language controls: what’s stored, why it’s used, and what you can change.
  • No selling identifiable financial data.
  • Change transparency: when policies change, we’ll publish the update with a clear date.
For details, see Privacy and Terms.

Planning tools should be honest about limitations.
  • Not advice: it doesn’t replace a licensed professional in your region.
  • No guarantees: projections are estimates, not outcomes.
  • You stay responsible: the plan supports decisions; it doesn’t make them.
  • Life changes: your plan should update when reality changes.

A few simple checks help prevent scams.
  • Verify the domain: official pages use trytesorin.com.
  • Don’t share one-time codes: no legitimate support needs your OTP.
  • Be careful with links: type the site directly if a message feels off.
  • Confirm claims: if it isn’t on the site, treat it as unverified.
